Causes of Climate Change

Climate change is primarily driven by the increase in greenhouse gases resulting from human activities. The main contributors are the burning of fossil fuels, which releases carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, causing heat to be trapped and global temperatures to rise. Deforestation further exacerbates the issue by reducing the planet's capacity to absorb carbon dioxide. Industrial processes and land use changes also play a significant role, emitting potent greenhouse gases like methane and nitrous oxide. Improper waste management and industrial emissions, along with natural factors such as volcanic eruptions and solar radiation variations, contribute to the problem to a lesser extent. Feedback loops, where the warming planet triggers self-reinforcing cycles, amplify the effects. However, the consensus within the scientific community overwhelmingly attributes the current rapid climate changes to human activities, emphasizing the urgency of transitioning to sustainable practices and policies globally.
