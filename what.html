What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to the long-term changes and shifts in the Earthâ€™s climate patterns resulting from human activities, especially the emission of greenhouse gasses such as Carbon Dioxide into the atmosphere. These gases confine heat, causing the planet to warm, leading to a range of environmental and societal complications. The predominant contributors to Climate Change include the burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, agricultural practices (livestock farming), etc. The consequences of Climate Change are far-reaching in the long term and can lead to increasing temperatures, severe storms, droughts, rising sea levels, loss of species, food insecurity, health risks, poverty, displacement, etc.
